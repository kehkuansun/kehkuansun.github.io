About me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics, Washington University in St. Louis. My primary fields of interest are behavioral and experimental economics, along with the secondary interests in microeconomic theory and economics of networks.

I come from a multicultural background as a descendant of a Chinese immigrant family in South Korea. Born and raised in a rural town of Korea, my journey began when I first arrived in Kansas as an exchange student in my high school days. The strange experience when I first recognized myself as an outsider of a community - and realized that I had always been an outsider even back in Korea - led me to the path of a nomad, later in Los Angeles, Beijing, Seoul, and finally St. Louis, the town I hold very dear.

I love new discoveries - a new language, different culture, something that resembles my father's old recipe, what my dog means when he scratches my closet door, to name a few.
